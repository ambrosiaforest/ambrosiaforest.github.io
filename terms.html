Sex, Gender, The body

Before you can start to understand the material from this course you first need to understand some basic terms. Many terms are similar to each other but have widely different meanings. "Personal identity is the way a person perceives themselves, while social identity is the way others perceive them."

Terms Definition
Gender identity How one expresses themselves in relation to their gender
Gender expression How one expresses their gender identity
Gender roles Roles that are stereotypically assigned to a gender
Gender binary The belief that there are 2 genders, male and female
Cisgender Someone whos gender identity alligns with their assigned sex at birth
Transgender Someone whos gender identity does not allign with their assigned sex at birth
Cisnormativity The notion that being cisgender is the "default"
Intersex Someone who is born with anatomical characteristics that are neither male nor female
Sexual orientation The direction of someones sexual interests
LGBTQ+ Stands for lesbian, gay, bisexual, transgender, and queer
Heteronormativity The notion that people are heterosexual by "default"
Asexual Someone who experiences little to no sexual attraction
Aromantic Someone who experiences little to none romatic attraction
Ableism Discrimination against disabilities
Genderism Discrimination based on gender
Classism Discrimination based on class
Feminism The movement that advocats for womens equality
Patriarchy A society where men are given advantages others are not
Misogyny Discrimination against women
Misogynoir A combination of racism and misogyny
Misandry Discrimination against men
Social Structures Norms created by society
Privilige Having opportunities that others may not have
Oppression Having less opportunities than others
